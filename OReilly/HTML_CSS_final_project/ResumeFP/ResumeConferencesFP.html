The following is a list of conferences and workshops I attended starting in 2010. During my management of the localization team (until April 2011), I explored changes in localization that could improve time to market and efficiencies. In 2009, I started to push for changes in the creation and management of the source content in order to solve issues that were affecting localization downstream. My goal was the adoption of processes and tools in English content creation, management, and production to positively impact localization in terms of cost, efficiencies, time to market, and accuracy.

With the reorganization of the department in November 2010 and the subsequent disbanding of the localization team at HQ in April 2011, I was moved into technical publications with the mandate to continue to investigate best practices, tools, and technologies to improve the English and localization content processes. At this time, I sought out and attended conferences in order to learn about tools, issues in the content industry, and solutions that other companies were employing.
